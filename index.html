How to play Murdoku

  1. The map is the crime scene. It is divided into colored rooms. Squares with furniture are blocked — nobody can stand there.
  2. Place every person — the suspects and the victim — on empty squares so that each row and each column contains exactly one person.
  3. Clues are rules, not hints. Every statement on every card must be true in your final layout. “Beside” means directly adjacent horizontally or vertically and in the same room — never diagonally, and never across a wall. “South of” means strictly lower on the map.
  4. Mark impossibilities. Use the ✕ tool (or right-click / long-press) to cross out squares a person can't occupy. Good detectives eliminate first.
  5. Find the murderer. When the layout is solved, the murderer is the person who ends up alone with the victim in the same room. Make your accusation!

Every case has exactly one solution, and none of them ever requires guessing — there is always a next deduction to find.

Keyboard: arrows move around the map · 1–9 select a person · Enter place · X cross out · M mark tool · H hint · Ctrl+Z/Y undo/redo · Esc cancel.
